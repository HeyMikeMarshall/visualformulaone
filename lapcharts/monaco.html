Grand Prix de Monaco
(Circuit de Monaco)

The Monaco Grand Prix, or Grand Prix de Monaco, is one of motor racing's premier events, and is considered by many to be one of the jewels in the unofficial 'Triple Crown of Motorsport'. It is a tight and winding track that runs through the streets of the Principality. Despite low average speeds, the sheer tightness of the circuit, combined with numerous blind corners and elevation changes, make for a dangerous race requiring intense focus from the drivers, simply to avoid crashing into a wall. The first Monaco Grand Prix was held in 1929, and it was one of 7 grand prix that made up the 1950 innagural season of the FIA World Championship of Drivers.


Rain in Monaco makes for an interesting race

Looking at this chart it's easy to see that between the 1996 and 2004 seasons, fastest lap times were falling each year. That is with the exception of the 1997 Monaco Grand Prix. In 1997, the weather led to a disasterous race for the majority of the grid. 12 of the 22 driver who qualified were forced to retire due to crashes, damage, or simply spinning off track and being unable to restart the car after stalling. Ultimately the 2 hour time limit was called on the race after 62 of 78 laps. Michael Schumacher won that race over 53 seconds ahead of the next car. 10 drivers finished that race, though only 4 would finish on the lead lap.
